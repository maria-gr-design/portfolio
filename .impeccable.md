## Design Context

### Users
Hiring managers and creative directors at design-forward companies, reviewing portfolios during recruitment. Primarily desktop but must work fully on mobile. They scan fast (under 60 seconds for first impression), look for visual quality, clear positioning, and evidence of senior-level thinking.

### Brand Personality
Calm confidence, precision, seniority. Not loud, not trendy, not trying to impress with effects. The design itself should demonstrate senior craft — every detail intentional.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Tone**: Minimal editorial with warmth. Light theme with warm neutrals.
- **Typography**: Satoshi (sans, body) + Sentient (serif, display). Already in use.
- **Palette**: Warm off-white (#F4F3F1), black text, tertiary grey (#888), subtle line color (#D4D2CD), dark footer (#1E1E1C).
- **Open question**: Whether to add an edgy/interactive element (scroll-driven reveals, subtle motion) to elevate beyond static editorial. Must feel intentional, not decorative.
- **Anti-references**: Generic AI-generated portfolios, Dribbble-trending dark mode neon sites, over-animated portfolio templates.

### Design Principles
1. **Show, don't declare**: The design quality IS the portfolio piece. Every spacing decision, type choice, and interaction should demonstrate senior-level craft.
2. **Earned confidence**: No flashy effects for their own sake. If there's motion or interactivity, it should serve navigation or comprehension.
3. **Scannable at speed**: A HM gives 30-60 seconds. Visual hierarchy must guide the eye without requiring effort.
4. **Accessible by default**: WCAG AA minimum. Responsive across all breakpoints. Consider additional accessibility options.
5. **Editorial restraint**: Magazine-like pacing — generous whitespace, clear sections, typography doing the heavy lifting.
